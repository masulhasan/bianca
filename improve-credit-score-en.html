Improving Your Credit Score: Insights from the Financial Consumer Agency of Canada 

Navigating the path to a better credit score is a crucial aspect of financial well-being. The Financial Consumer Agency of Canada offers valuable tips to enhance your credit standing.

Monitoring Your Payment History

Your payment history holds the key to your credit score.
To boost it:

1. Ensure timely payments.
2. Make at least the minimum payment if the full amount is challenging.
3. Reach out to your lender if payment issues arise.
4. Avoid skipping payments, even if a bill is in dispute.

Receive electronic alerts from your financial institution to manage your credit payments effectively.

Using Credit Wisely

Prudent credit use is pivotal for a healthy score:

1. Stay within your credit limit.
2. Aim to use less than 30% of your available credit.
3. Having a higher credit limit while using less reflects positively on your score.
4. Lenders view high credit usage as a risk, regardless of full, timely payments.

Improving Your Credit History

Longevity matters; the longer a credit account is open, the better your score. Avoid transferring older accounts, as credit bureaus treat the new account as a new credit. Keep older accounts active, use them periodically, and check for fees if you plan to keep them open.

Limiting Credit Applications and Checks

Credit inquiries impact your score; manage them wisely:

1. Limit credit applications.
2. Obtain quotes from different lenders within a 2-week period to consolidate inquiries.
3. Apply for credit only when necessary.
4. Distinguish between "hard hits" and "soft hits" – the former affects your score, while the latter is visible only to you.

Diversifying Your Credit

A mix of credit types positively influences your score:

1. Combine credit card usage with a car loan or a line of credit.
2. Diversification can improve your credit score, but always ensure you can meet repayment obligations.

Empower yourself with these strategies to not only improve but also maintain a healthy credit score. Your financial journey deserves the benefits of a strong credit foundation.
