FAQ

Why work with a mortgage broker?

A mortgage broker can provide you with expert advice and support throughout the mortgage process. They can explain the different types of loans, the pros and cons of each one, and the requirements and qualifications for each one. They can also help you prepare your application, gather and verify your documents, and submit your loan to the lender. They can also monitor the status of your loan, troubleshoot any issues, and ensure a smooth and timely closing.

What is the difference between a mortgage broker and the bank?

The main difference between a mortgage broker and a bank is that a mortgage broker can offer you multiple loan options from different lenders, while a bank can only offer you its own products. A mortgage broker can help you compare and choose the best loan for your situation, negotiate the best terms and rates, and guide you through the application and closing process. A bank can give you more control and lower fees, but may have fewer choices and stricter requirements.

How does a mortgage broker get paid?

A mortgage broker is a professional who helps you find the best loan for your situation, negotiate the best terms and rates, and guide you through the application and closing process. A mortgage broker gets paid by a commission from the lender or the borrower, but not both. The commission is usually a percentage of the loan amount, ranging from 1% to 2%. The commission can be paid upfront or added to the loan balance. 

What are the benefits of working with a mortgage broker?

Working with a mortgage broker can save you time, money, and hassle. A mortgage broker can access a wide network of lenders and find the most suitable and competitive options for you. A mortgage broker can also negotiate better deals, advise you on how to improve your credit and avoid pitfalls, and handle the communication and coordination with the lenders and other parties involved in the transaction.
